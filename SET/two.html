Mahanadi Ali, Syria, 32 years old
Canada was my only hope 2 years ago when I was living as a refugee in Germany. Most of my family members died at war. Me and my younger brother were alone in Germany. We had some support from the government, but obviously it was not enough to have a descent quality of life. Without the German language it was impossible to find a good job, so, we decided to apply for the Canadian visa and hope that God would hear our prayers. We found a few companies that were ready to help us, but only Solid Visa was previously dealing with refugee cases. As we didn’t have any documents accept refugee cards, this argument was a decision maker for us.
Make Your Own Success Story
Getting a Canadian visa turned out to be a long and complicated process. It also cost money: we had to borrow some of it from our Syrian relatives. The company kindly allowed us to pay little by little, so in a year, after we paid the full sum, both me and my brother got a Canadian work permit as family members. We haven’t received any special education back in Syria, so I went working at the farm, where the owner trusted me with almost every kind of work. My brother, meanwhile, was hired by logistical company as an English-Arabic interpreter (he could speak much better English than me at that time). It was so surprising, that in less than 3 month we paid back all of the money we borrowed from our relatives. In 5 month I became a chief of this farm I was working at, as the owner opened a new one. And my brother started working as a manager in international relations department at that logistical company. Now, after a year here we can afford renting an apartment in the center of Toronto, we bought a car and we even go to the restaurants from time to time. I could never ever imagine I would live this kind of life, as in Syria I was desperate and broke. I believe everybody de- serves to be appreciated, to earn good money and to enjoy the high quality life.
